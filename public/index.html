C H A R   D H A M

Uttarakhand, also known as Devbhoomi or the Land of Gods, is renowned for its multitude of temples and attracts pilgrims throughout the year. A significant pilgrimage route in this region is the Char Dham Yatra, which includes visits to four sacred sites: Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath, and Badrinath. These revered destinations are nestled high in the Himalayas. In Hindi 'char' means four, and 'dham' signifies holy places.

The Char Dham temples, situated at the high elevations, remain closed for approximately six months each year. They generally reopen in the summer (April or May) and shut again as winter approaches (October or November). Pilgrims are advised to undertake the Char Dham Yatra in a clockwise order: beginning with Yamunotri, then proceeding to Gangotri, followed by Kedarnath, and concluding at Badrinath. Travelers can choose to reach these sites by road or use helicopter services. Additionally, some choose a shorter pilgrimage, known as the Do Dham Yatra, visiting only Kedarnath and Badrinath.

what makes uttarakhand special

National Parks

Uttarakhand features 6 National Parks (including Jim Corbett, India’s first national park), 7 Wildlife Sanctuaries, 4 Conservation Reserves, and 1 Biosphere Reserve.

Kumbh Mela

The Kumbh Mela in Haridwar, Uttarakhand, is a major Hindu festival held every 12 years. It attracts millions of devotees to bathe in the sacred Ganges River and features grand processions, spiritual rituals, and vibrant cultural celebrations.

Char Dham Yatra

This annual pilgrimage to the four holy temples in Uttarakhand—Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath, and Badrinath—is regarded as one of the most sacred in Hinduism and attracts millions of devotees.

Land of Gods

Uttarakhand, known as Devbhoomi (Land of the Gods), is home to sacred sites like Char Dham, Panch Kedar, Panch Badri, and Panch Prayag.

Yoga Capital of the World

Rishikesh, along the River Ganges, is the Yoga Capital of the World. Each March, it hosts the International Yoga Festival, drawing enthusiasts, practitioners, and gurus from around the globe.

Adventure Hub

Uttarakhand offers a wealth of adventure activities, including white water rafting, paragliding, bungee jumping, trekking, skiing, and mountain biking. Auli is renowned as India's skiing paradise, while Rishikesh boasts the country's highest bungee platform!
